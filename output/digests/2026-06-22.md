Today's theme: The transition from static AI models to "living systems" that evolve through continuous learning and real-world interaction.

### From Static Models to Living Systems via Continual Learning
**What the video argues:** Ronak Malde of Trajectory.ai argues that the next frontier for AI products is moving away from static, frozen models toward "living systems." These are systems that utilize continual learning to adapt and improve dynamically based on real-world user interactions rather than relying solely on periodic, massive retraining cycles.

**Key details:**
* **Self-Distillation Policy Optimization (SDPO):** A specialized algorithm designed to accelerate the training of models by using "privileged information" and hints to help student models learn from teacher models more effectively in a scaled environment.
* **Product-Centric Accessibility:** Trajectory.ai aims to move continual learning out of the research lab and into the hands of product managers, providing them with the observability tools and abstraction layers needed to manage model evolution.
* **Domain-Specific Applications:** The framework is being applied to high-stakes environments like legal services, healthcare, and coding, where accuracy must stay current with evolving regulations and technical standards.

**Why it matters:** This shifts the value proposition of AI from a "one-time deployment" to a "continuous optimization" model. For enterprises, this means products can become more accurate over time without requiring constant manual intervention or massive data re-processing cycles.
