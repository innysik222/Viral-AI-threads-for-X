1. General relativity from first principles – Adam Brown

What the video argues:
General relativity, Einstein's masterpiece, explains gravity as the curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy. It unifies time dilation, black holes, and the most beautiful theory ever conceived.

Key details:
- Equivalence Principle: Mass equals gravitational charge.
- Spacetime Curvature: Gravity is a result of massive objects curving space-time.
- Event Horizons & Redshifts: Time slows down near black holes, causing light to shift towards the red.

More context:
The Equivalence Principle: Adam Brown explains that mass and gravitational charge are equivalent. This pivotal insight led Einstein to conclude that gravity is not a force but an effect of curved spacetime. Spacetime Curvature: General relativity posits that massive objects cause space-time to curve, leading to the phenomenon we experience as gravity. This curvature affects not only motion but also time itself-making it slower near massive bodies like black holes. Event Horizons & Redshifts: Near a black hole's event horizon, time appears to slow down significantly for an outside observer. This effect is known as gravitational time dilation and causes light emitted from the vicinity of the event horizon to shift towards the red part of the spectrum-a phenomenon called gravitational redshift.
