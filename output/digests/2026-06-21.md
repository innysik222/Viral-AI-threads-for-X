Today's theme: The transition from AI-assisted tools to autonomous diagnostic infrastructure in healthcare.

**Automated Radiology and the Future of Primary Care**
What the video argues: Qure.ai is moving beyond "human-in-the-loop" models toward fully automated diagnostics for high-volume screenings like tuberculosis and lung cancer. Prashant Warier suggests that primary care will transition into an AI-driven model within a decade, where AI handles initial diagnoses and test recommendations to free up doctors for complex cases.

Key details:
* Qure.ai’s algorithms currently process 15 million X-rays annually without human intervention, specifically targeting regions with radiologist shortages.
* The system provides tuberculosis results in under 20 seconds, allowing for immediate clinical action in underserved areas.
* A lung nodule malignancy risk score improved detection rates from 2 out of 100 to 54 out of 100, accelerating the timeline for necessary follow-up CT scans by over 60 days.

Why it matters: This represents a shift from AI as a "decision support" tool to AI as autonomous infrastructure; by removing human bottlenecks in initial screenings, healthcare systems can drastically reduce diagnostic latency and scale care in underserved regions.
