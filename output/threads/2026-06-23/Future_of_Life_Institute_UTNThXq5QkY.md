### 🧠 Deep Dive

#### **1. Sputnik and Space Race (1957-1963)**
In the early 1960s, the space race between the United States and the Soviet Union pushed technology beyond its political boundaries. The launch of Sputnik in 1957 marked a turning point. The General Assembly recognized that outer space should not be claimed by any nation, leading to the Outer Space Treaty (1967). This treaty effectively banned orbiting nuclear weapons, ensuring that no single country could dominate or threaten with such technology.

#### **2. Biological Weapons and Nixon’s Decision (1960s-1970)**
During the Cold War, both superpowers developed sophisticated biological weapons programs. However, in 1969, President Richard Nixon decided to renounce all forms of deadly biological weapons. This decision was not driven by public pressure but rather a Defense Department review that found no military advantage and risks of proliferation. The push for a Biological Weapons Convention (BWC) followed, eventually leading to the first multilateral treaty in history to ban an entire class of weapons.

#### **3. Ozone Layer and CFCs (1970s-1980s)**
In 1974, chemists Sherwood Rowland and Mario Molina published groundbreaking research on chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs). Their findings revealed that these chemicals could destroy the ozone layer. Despite skepticism from industry giants like DuPont, international negotiations led by Mustafa Tolba resulted in the Montreal Protocol in 1987. This treaty has been ratified by every nation and is now showing signs of success as the ozone layer recovers.
