## 🔬 Finding 1: The "Local vs. Global" Verbs Bottleneck

**What's happening:** Current AI architectures (transformers, diffusion models) are masters of *local* optimization — pattern matching within closed datasets. AlphaFold solves protein folding. Next-token prediction writes code. But we lack the mathematical "verbs" to perform **theoretical archaeology** on 100M+ parameter black boxes.

We can't yet crack open a trained model and extract universal principles. Like trying to derive General Relativity by staring at gravitational data without calculus.

**Why it matters:** Interpretability is the chokepoint. Until we can distill large models into fundamental laws, AI won't fully accelerate science. We're building powerful engines without understanding their blueprints.

---

## 🛸 Finding 2: Non-Convergent Tech Stack Path Dependency

**What's happening:** Technology trees are contingent — not inevitable. They depend on biological priors: our visual cortex, our motor constraints, our environmental pressures.

An alien civilization wouldn't land on our tech stack. They'd explore orthogonal branches entirely.

**Why it matters:** Generalization to "alien problem spaces" isn't guaranteed by scaling. Our AI is locked to human sensory embeddings. True cross-domain intelligence requires modeling *divergent cognitive priors* — not just feeding more data.

---

## ⚠️ Finding 3: The "Muddy System" Learning Trap

**What's happening:** AI-assisted learning optimizes for navigating "balls of mud" — complex legacy codebases, the Linux kernel. Pattern recognition fills the gap.

But this eliminates **productive frustration** — the high-variance stuckness that forces you to forge *transferable mental models*.

**Why it matters:** Risk of training a generation of engineers who can navigate complexity but can't derive first principles. The cognitive primitives for novel scientific leaps *require struggle*. Remove friction, remove depth.
