## 1. 🧠 The "Local vs. Global" Verbs Bottleneck

AI crushes **local** optimization—AlphaFold folding proteins, GPT generating text—mapping patterns within closed datasets. But extracting *universal principles* from 100M+ parameter black boxes? That's a different game entirely. We lack the mathematical "verbs" to perform theoretical archaeology on our own models.

> **Why it matters:** Until we develop interpretability tools that distill complex systems into fundamental laws, AI will remain a powerful oracle, not a collaborator in discovery. The next physics breakthrough may require operators we haven't invented yet.

---

## 2. 🌌 Non-Convergent Tech Stack Path Dependency

The "tech tree" isn't a ladder—it's an infinitely branching bush, contingent on **biological priors** (vision, hearing, motor control). Alien AI wouldn't be "more advanced." It would be exploring entirely different branches.

> **Why it matters:** Our current AI trajectories are locked to human sensory and environmental constraints. This path dependency limits generalization to "alien problem spaces." If we ever meet non-human intelligence, interoperability is far from guaranteed.

---

## 3. ⚠️ The "Muddy System" Learning Trap

AI accelerates mastery of "balls of mud" (Linux kernels, legacy codebases). But by eliminating **high-variance stuckness**—deep frustration and struggle—AI-assisted learning optimizes for input-output mapping over *transferable understanding*.

> **Why it matters:** We may be training a generation of engineers who can navigate complexity but can't derive first principles. The cognitive primitives needed for novel scientific leaps are forged through productive struggle. Remove the struggle, and you may remove the leap.
