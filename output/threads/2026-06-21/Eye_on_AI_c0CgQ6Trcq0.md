# AI-Powered X-Rays: The Future of Healthcare Is Here

**TL;DR:** Qure.ai's cutting-edge algorithms are already reading 15 million x-rays a year, marking a significant shift in healthcare diagnostics towards full automation.

### 🔑 Key Insights
*   **Automation of Primary Care:** Prashant Warier envisions primary care becoming largely automated within the next decade.
*   **TB Screening Automation:** Qure.ai's system can interpret chest x-rays without human intervention, crucial in regions lacking radiologists.
*   **Early Detection and Reporting:** The company’s tools significantly improve reporting times for lung cancer detection by over 60 days.

### 🧠 Deep Dive
#### Automation of Primary Care

Prashant Warier believes that primary care is moving towards full automation. "Primary care will be AI in the future, maybe 5 to 10 years from now," he states. This shift involves using AI to diagnose basic conditions and recommend tests, potentially freeing up doctors for more complex cases.

#### TB Screening Automation

In regions with a shortage of radiologists, Qure.ai's system plays a critical role. "Today the protocol there is that x-ray gets taken, gets automatically uploaded to our laptop," Warier explains. Within 20 seconds, the algorithm determines whether the x-ray is positive for tuberculosis, enabling quick action.

#### Early Detection and Reporting

The CREATE study highlights Qure.ai's ability to enhance early detection. By using a lung nodule malignancy risk score, the company can identify patients who need follow-up CT scans. "We are going from two out of 100 to 54 out of 100 with this lung nodule malignancy risk score," Warier notes. This improvement in early detection could save countless lives.

### 🏁 Conclusion
Qure.ai's advancements are reshaping healthcare by leveraging AI for diagnostics. While automation brings efficiency and accuracy, it also presents challenges in patient communication and trust. The path towards fully automated primary care is clear, but the journey requires careful implementation to ensure patient safety and satisfaction.
