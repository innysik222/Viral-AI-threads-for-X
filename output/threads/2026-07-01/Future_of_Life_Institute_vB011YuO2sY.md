## 📰 SUMMARY ARTICLE

# AI's Impact on Children’s Critical Thinking Skills: AFT President Randy Wearten Sounds the Alarm

**TL;DR:** The American Federation of Teachers (AFT) president, Randy Wearten, warns that screens and AI are harming children's ability to think critically. He calls for a ban on student-facing AI in elementary schools, emphasizing the need for active learning and traditional pen-and-paper methods.

### 🔑 Key Insights
*   **Ban on Screens K-2:** Early exposure to tech is deemed detrimental.
*   **Student-Facing AI Restricted:** Use of AI companions for children should be banned.
*   **Active Learning Emphasis:** Promotes project-based and experiential learning over passive knowledge acquisition.

### 🧠 Deep Dive
#### The Dangers of Screens K-2

Randy Wearten, the president of the American Federation of Teachers (AFT), highlights that screens in early education have a negative impact on children's attention spans and cognitive abilities. "The test scores in America and the world have gone down from the time that screens have become really prevalent," he notes, suggesting a direct correlation between screen exposure and reduced academic performance.

#### The Role of AI

Wearten also raises concerns about how AI is being used in education, particularly among younger students. He warns against cognitive offloading—students relying on AI to complete tasks instead of developing their own critical thinking skills. "If you are just a prompt away from lots of knowledge, then how do you actually assess whether kids have learned anything?" he asks.

#### Active Learning vs. Passive Knowledge

The AFT president advocates for active learning methods such as project-based instruction and debate. He emphasizes that traditional teaching techniques like pen-and-paper should be reintroduced to ensure students develop the necessary cognitive skills. "Active learning means project-based learning, experiential learning, meaning the kinds of things where you have to take several different steps from beginning to end," Wearten explains.

#### Ethical Use of AI

Wearten stresses the importance of ensuring that AI is used ethically and responsibly in education. He calls for gold standards for AI safety and privacy, advocating for transparency and parent control over their children's data. "You need to have ways of ensuring that people are learning from each other," he states.

#### Expert Quotes
> "If you're actually doing so much more with active learning, if we're actually understanding that things like music and art and sports and debate and career tech ed projects, if we understand that these kind of things that we see as either endterm capstone projects or this is the way in which classrooms are organized I then I think you'll see a lot more vibrancy and more importantly you'll see a lot more agency for children," Wearten concludes.

### 💬 Notable Quotes

### 🏁 Conclusion
Randy Wearten's warnings about the potential harm AI poses to children's critical thinking skills are timely and crucial. As technology continues to play an increasingly prominent role in education, it is essential that policymakers, educators, and parents work together to ensure that our educational systems prioritize active learning and the development of core cognitive abilities.
