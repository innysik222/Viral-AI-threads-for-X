## 📰 SUMMARY ARTICLE

# The '100,000 Agent Problem' and How Barndoor AI is Solving It

**TL;DR:** Enterprises are facing a significant challenge as they integrate AI agents into their operations, but Barndoor AI offers a solution by providing governance layers that ensure these agents operate safely and effectively.

### 🔑 Key Insights
*   **Agents as Enthusiastic Interns**: Agents are probabilistic systems that can make mistakes, much like enthusiastic interns.
*   **Trust and Governance Issues**: Enterprises are cautious due to the inherent risks associated with AI's probabilistic nature.
*   **MCP Protocol**: While MCP allows for better integration of AI into enterprise tools, it doesn't address trust issues comprehensively.

### 🧠 Deep Dive

#### The Probabilistic Nature of Agents
Oren Michaels explains that agents are akin to enthusiastic interns—they will do what they're told but can make mistakes. "Agents are probabilistic systems," he notes. "They give you the most likely answer, which is different from deterministic APIs where a program does exactly the same thing every time."

#### Trust and Governance Challenges
The core issue enterprises face is trust. Oren states that companies need to govern agents effectively without stifling their potential. He explains: "In order to really trust an agent to act autonomously, you basically have to govern that agent to a point where you're giving the agent a task and the only things it's allowed to do are the tasks related to that task."

#### The Role of Barndoor AI
Barndoor AI addresses these governance issues by providing a platform that manages agents across various enterprise tools. Oren elaborates: "What we built at Barnor is this governance layer that manages all of these different agents and on behalf of humans or not on behalf of humans, what the agents are allowed to do given the tools they're trying to use, the context, the data they're acting on, what specific task they're doing."

#### OpenClaw and MCP
While Oren acknowledges that protocols like MCP have made significant progress in connecting AI to enterprise systems, he argues that these don't fully address trust. "MCP is relatively new and it has taken off a bit on the developer set because it's kind of a technical thing to get up and running," he says. "Your average finance HR salesperson isn't really doing much with it yet."

#### Practical Application
Barn Door offers tools like Ven, which allows individuals to experiment with connecting AI to their personal tools. Oren explains: "The idea is that you as an individual can come to ven.ai and sign up, get started for free and you can use it to hook up to all those same things, email, Slack, you know, uh, calendar, all the different tools you use."
