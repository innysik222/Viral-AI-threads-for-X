# Inside xAI: Building Grok Imagine in 3 Months, Videogen vs World Models, and Video Agents— Ethan He (2026-06-02)

**TL;DR:** In just three months, xAI developed the groundbreaking Grok Imagine AI. Key insights include the critical role of prompt rewriting, the future of video agents, and the growing importance of language models in generative media.

### 🔑 Key Insights
*   **Prompt Rewriting Matters Most:** The majority of improvements in video generation come from better language models rather than just diffusion technology.
*   **Video Agents Are Coming Soon:** They will enable iterative refinement using tools like FFMPEG and Photoshop, leading to production-grade videos.
*   **Context-Aware Models:** Future research might involve infinite context and self-modifying AI harnesses.

### 🧠 Deep Dive
**Rapid Development at xAI:**
Ethan He, formerly of xAI, explains how the company's culture of "move fast" allowed rapid development. The team focused on prompt rewriting and leveraging powerful language models to drive video generation improvements. These advancements, particularly in natural language processing, are more impactful than just refining diffusion models.

**Video Agents:**
He predicts a significant shift towards video agents in the coming year. Video agents will use tools like FFMPEG and Photoshop to iteratively create high-quality videos. This approach combines generative AI with traditional editing techniques, enabling sophisticated and detailed outputs that surpass what current standalone AI can achieve.

**Future Research Directions:**
Ethan envisions context-aware models that can manage their own context length and self-modify in real-time. He believes these models will be crucial for future advancements in both language and video generation technologies.

### 💬 Notable Quotes
> "The majority of the gain comes from the language model, not just the video or diffusion model." - Ethan He
