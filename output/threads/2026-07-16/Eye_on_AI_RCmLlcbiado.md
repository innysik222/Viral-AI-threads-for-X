# The AI Factory Reality: Why Enterprises Struggle to Find Root Causes of Failure

**TL;DR:** A new study reveals that 60% of enterprises struggle to identify the root cause when their AI workloads fail. The key issue lies in the complexity of AI infrastructure and a lack of real-time observability.

### 🔑 Key Insights
- **60% of Enterprises Struggle with Root Cause Identification**: Enterprises face significant challenges in pinpointing the exact issues causing failures in AI workloads.
- **Complexity of AI Infrastructures**: The increasing complexity of AI systems, including data pipelines and orchestration layers, makes it harder to identify root causes.
- **Operational Efficiency Takes Center Stage**: As companies scale their AI investments, the focus is shifting from model performance to operational efficiency and business impact.

### 🧠 Deep Dive
**The Problem of Root Cause Identification**
According to Paul Appleby, CEO of Virtana, "60% of enterprises can't automatically identify the root cause across AI infrastructure domains when AI workloads fail." This issue arises due to the complexity of modern AI systems. "AI factories" now involve a myriad of components, including data pipelines, AI workloads, and underlying compute and storage infrastructure. Stitching together this complex web of data is a significant challenge for IT professionals.

**The Role of Observability**
To address these challenges, Appleby emphasizes the importance of real-time observability. "We've been building deep AI and ML capabilities to identify threats in real time," he says. Virtana's platform captures 20,000 different metrics sub-second and uses advanced models for correlation and causality analysis. This real-time visibility is crucial for timely remediation and ensuring system resilience.

**Shift in AI ROI**
Another significant finding from the study is that companies are increasingly focusing on operational efficiency and business impact rather than just model performance when evaluating their AI investments. Appleby notes, "We're entering a phase where AI ROI will be judged less by model performance and more by operational efficiency—cost and business impact." This shift underscores the importance of holistic governance and controls in managing AI infrastructure.
