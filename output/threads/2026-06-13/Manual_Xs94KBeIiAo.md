# Graham Hancock’s Revolutionary Theory: A Lost Civilization 20,000 Years Ago?

**TL;DR:** Archaeologist and author Graham Hancock proposes a lost civilization that thrived 20,000 years ago, challenging traditional views on human history.

### 🔑 Key Insights
*   **Ancient Myths & Maps**: Hancock argues that myths and maps from around the world hint at a forgotten era.
*   **Comet Impact Hypothesis**: A massive comet storm 12,800 years ago caused a global cataclysm that wiped out much of humanity.
*   **Advanced Knowledge in Pyramids**: The Great Pyramid of Giza incorporates knowledge believed to be impossible for its time.

### 🧠 Deep Dive

#### Lost Civilization Hypothesis
Hancock posits that there was an advanced civilization 20,000 years ago. This civilization is described as a "golden age" where humans lived without violence and had sophisticated technologies such as navigation and astronomy (Hancock, 2026).

#### Cataclysmic Events
According to Hancock’s theory, around 12,800 years ago, the Earth faced a catastrophic event. He cites the “younger dryas” impact hypothesis, suggesting that a comet storm caused massive changes in climate and even led to the extinction of many large mammals (Hancock, 2026).

#### The Great Pyramid
The Great Pyramid of Giza is a central piece of evidence for Hancock’s theory. He argues that its construction included precise astronomical alignments and encoded information about Earth's dimensions, which were supposedly beyond the knowledge available at that time (Hancock, 2026). This challenges conventional views on ancient Egyptian civilization.

#### Lessons for Modern Society
Hancock believes that we are currently living in a time of high consciousness, similar to what these lost civilizations may have faced. He emphasizes the importance of understanding our interconnectedness with nature and each other (Hancock, 2026).
