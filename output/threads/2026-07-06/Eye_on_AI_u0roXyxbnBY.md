### 🧠 Deep Dive
#### The Problem: High Failure Rates in Late-Stage Clinical Trials

Dr. Vin Singh, founder and CEO of BullFrog AI, explains that the high failure rate of drugs at late-stage clinical trials is a significant problem for the pharmaceutical industry. "Considering it takes 10 to 15 years and 1 to 2 billion dollars to develop a drug," he says, "it's quite shocking that big pharma fails 50% of the time in phase three." This failure rate translates into enormous financial losses and missed opportunities for patients.

#### The Solution: AI-Driven Drug Discovery

BullFrog AI has developed advanced AI technologies designed to improve the success rates of drug development. "We discovered the biological drivers of those diseases," Singh says, referring to their breakthrough in identifying genes that drive conditions like depression, bipolar disorder, and schizophrenia. Their technology includes:
1. **BF Prep:** A platform for preparing messy data sets.
2. **BF Leap:** An analytics engine using causal AI to identify drug targets.
3. **BF Arenas:** A tool for prioritizing the best options among potential drug targets.

#### The Process: Partnering with Big Pharma

Big pharma companies are interested in partnering with BullFrog AI, but the process is complex and time-consuming. "The sales cycle can be over a year," Singh explains, "and it's often about proving that our technology works." Pilot projects are essential to establish trust and validate the technology before larger deals.

#### The Future: Precision Medicine and Beyond

Singh envisions a future where AI-driven technologies like those developed by BullFrog AI will transform drug development. "We want to be a revenue-generating company, not a long-term drug developer," he says. This strategy focuses on delivering tangible results for their pharma partners while maintaining innovation.
