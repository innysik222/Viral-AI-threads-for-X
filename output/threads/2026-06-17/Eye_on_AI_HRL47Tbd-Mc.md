# India Becomes the Architect of Global AI Order: An Insider's Perspective

**TL;DR:** India is no longer just a service center for AI but an architect of global policies and practices, focusing on ethical AI and inclusion. Wipro’s executive Ivana Bartoletti discusses this shift.

### 🔑 Key Insights
*   **India as a Trusted AI Architect**: Prime Minister Modi's summit showcased responsible AI with strong ethical standards.
*   **Inclusion Focus**: AI is being used to include the broader population, not just tier-one cities.
*   **Unique Strengths**: Large population, massive data production, and robust digital infrastructure.

### 🧠 Deep Dive

#### India’s Strategic Shift
At the 2026 AI summit in New Delhi, Wipro's Ivana Bartoletti highlighted that India is no longer just a consumer or talent exporter for AI; it has become an architect of global policies. The summit emphasized responsible and inclusive AI practices, reflecting India’s commitment to ethical standards.

#### Ethical AI and Inclusion
Prime Minister Modi stated, "There's no compromise on ethical AI," underscoring the country’s dedication to inclusivity and human-centric AI development. This approach is particularly significant as it targets the needs of a diverse population, including rural areas, where digital infrastructure has been rapidly expanding.

#### Unique Strengths
India boasts unique strengths that make it an ideal hub for AI:
- **Population**: With over 1.4 billion people, India generates vast amounts of data and talent.
- **Data Production**: Produces 20% of global data and has a high mobile penetration rate.
- **Digital Infrastructure**: Successful digital public infrastructure initiatives like UPI have scaled up quickly.

#### Wipro's Role
Wipro, under Ivana Bartoletti’s leadership, is shifting from traditional services to consulting and innovation. The company aims to leverage its unique strengths in skills training and experimentation to become a trusted AI partner globally. This shift reflects India’s broader strategy of developing responsible and inclusive AI solutions.

#### Vertical vs. Horizontal AI
India's focus on vertical applications rather than horizontal LLMs indicates strategic thinking around real-world impact. Wipro is working on specific use cases that can benefit Indian society, such as healthcare and manufacturing, while also investing in data center infrastructure to support these initiatives.

#### Skills Gap and Training
While India has a significant tech talent pool, there is still a skills gap. Companies like Wipro are investing heavily in training programs to upskill their workforce and ensure they have the necessary technical expertise for advanced AI projects.

### 💬 Notable Quotes

> "India is becoming an architect of the global AI order," says Ivana Bartoletti, Vice President at Wipro.
>
> "We need to focus on scaling AI everywhere, not just in tier-one cities," adds Bartoletti.

### 🏁 Conclusion
India’s strategic push into ethical and inclusive AI is reshaping the global landscape. As a trusted partner for responsible AI development, India aims to lead the charge in the global south while maintaining strong ties with international partners like Europe and the US. Follow Wipro's journey as they navigate this evolving landscape.
