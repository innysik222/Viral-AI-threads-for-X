# Notion’s Co-Founder Reveals the Agent-First Future of Engineering

**TL;DR:**  In a recent interview, Notion co-founder Simon Last revealed a dramatic shift in the company’s approach to AI: moving from building tools for humans to building tools *for* AI agents. This transition necessitates a fundamental rethinking of engineering roles, requiring a focus on agent management and continuous adaptation to the rapidly evolving AI landscape.  Notion’s aggressive indexing strategy and embrace of agent-driven workflows are positioning it at the forefront of this transformative change.

### 🔑 Key Insights

*   **Agent-First Paradigm:** Notion’s strategy has evolved from building tools for humans to creating tools and frameworks to empower AI agents.
*   **Rapid Iteration is Key:** Notion rewrites its AI harness every six months, highlighting the need for constant adaptation and experimentation in the fast-moving AI space.
*   **The Rise of Agent Managers:** Traditional coding roles are evolving into “agent manager” positions, demanding a new skillset focused on overseeing and optimizing AI-driven workflows.
*   **Data Indexing as a Competitive Advantage:** Notion’s aggressive indexing strategy, tackling problems other platforms have struggled with, provides a significant edge.
*   **The Importance of Empirical Evaluation:** Notion emphasizes the need for continuous experimentation and data-driven evaluation to refine AI agent performance and ensure alignment with user needs.

### 🧠 Deep Dive

**The Agent-First Shift: A Paradigm Change**

Simon Last’s insights reveal a profound shift in Notion’s technological direction. Initially focused on creating a robust and user-friendly productivity suite for humans, the company has now embraced a future where AI agents play a central role. This isn't merely about integrating AI features; it’s a fundamental reimagining of how work gets done.  As Last states, “Our goal was to create the best tool for humans to directly perform their work. Now the goal is to create the best tool for humans to manage agents to do the work for them.” This change necessitates a new architecture and a different mindset.

**The Importance of Rapid Iteration and the AI Harness**

The pace of innovation in the AI space is relentless. Notion’s commitment to rewriting its AI harness every six months underscores the importance of continuous adaptation.  This isn’t about minor tweaks; it’s a fundamental restructuring to leverage new model capabilities and optimize performance.  "We were rewriting our AI harness probably every six months or so," Last explains. This willingness to abandon and rebuild highlights the inherent instability of early-stage AI development and the need for a flexible and adaptable engineering approach.

**The Evolution of Engineering Roles: From Coder to Agent Manager**

The rise of AI agents is fundamentally reshaping engineering roles. Traditional coding skills remain valuable, but the ability to orchestrate and manage AI-driven workflows is becoming increasingly crucial.  Simon Last’s own transition from coder to “agent manager” exemplifies this shift. This new role requires a deeper understanding of AI capabilities, a focus on data quality and verification, and the ability to design and optimize complex agent-driven systems.  As Last observes, “The minimum bar has not changed, but the maximum bar has *extremely* increased."

**Notion’s Aggressive Indexing Strategy: Solving a Persistent Problem**

Notion’s decision to aggressively index data across various platforms – Slack, Google Drive, and more – reveals a strategic focus on solving a persistent pain point.  As Last points out, "most companies are pretty bad at making their indexes work." This willingness to tackle challenges that others have avoided positions Notion as a leader in AI-powered productivity and demonstrates a commitment to providing a comprehensive and integrated user experience.

Notion’s journey from a productivity tool to an agent-first platform provides a compelling glimpse into the future of work. The shift demands a new skillset, a relentless focus on iteration, and a willingness to embrace the transformative power of AI. As AI agents become increasingly integrated into our workflows, the ability to manage and orchestrate these agents will be a critical differentiator, and Notion appears poised to lead the way.
