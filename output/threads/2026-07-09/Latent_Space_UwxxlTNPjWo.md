## 📰 SUMMARY ARTICLE

# The Evolution of Modal Tech: From Developer Experience to Agent Experience

**TL;DR:** Modal CTO Akshat Bubna discusses the shift from developer experience (DX) to agent experience (AX), emphasizing that as AI scales, tools must adapt. Key takeaways include the importance of self-provisioning sandboxes and RDM networking.

### 🔑 Key Insights
*   **Agent Experience Over Developer Experience:** Modal’s focus has shifted towards improving the experience for agents rather than developers.
*   **Self-Provisioning Sandboxes & RDM Networking:** These innovations streamline workflows, especially in bursty workloads like RL training.
*   **Observability Remains Crucial:** Despite advancements, observability still plays a vital role in managing AI applications.

### 🧠 Deep Dive
**From Developer Experience to Agent Experience**
Modal Tech has transformed its approach by placing agent experience at the forefront. Akshat Bubna, CTO of Modal, explains that the company now considers how agents interact with tools and platforms rather than just focusing on developer convenience. This shift is crucial as AI applications grow in complexity and scale.

**Self-Provisioning Sandboxes & RDM Networking**
A key innovation at Modal is self-provisioning sandboxes, which allow for rapid changes and iterations without manual intervention. Additionally, RDM networking enhances performance in tasks like RL training by bypassing the traditional TCP stack. These primitives are designed to be agent-native, providing a seamless experience that aligns with the needs of modern AI applications.

**The Role of Observability**
Despite advancements in self-provisioning and RDM networking, observability remains essential. Modal’s products still offer robust dashboards for monitoring, allowing humans to interpret data and make informed decisions. This balance between automation and human oversight ensures that even as tools become more sophisticated, there is a clear path for intervention when necessary.

### 💬 Notable Quotes
> "Would you have an agent read through hundreds of Kubernetes files and write YAML that's not even typed when it can basically make a couple changes in a decorator and it gets this sort of self-provisioning runtime of being able to see its changes live in action?" - Akshat Bubna, Modal CTO

### 🏁 Conclusion
As AI applications continue to grow in complexity, tools must evolve alongside them. Modal’s focus on agent experience is a forward-looking approach that ensures the technology remains accessible and user-friendly. The future of AI management lies in tools that can handle the vast scale and burstiness of modern workloads while maintaining human oversight.
