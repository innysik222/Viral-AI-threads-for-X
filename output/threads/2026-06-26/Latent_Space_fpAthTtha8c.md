# Pre-Training Isn't Dead: Mark Chen's Vision for OpenAI’s Future

**TL;DR:** Mark Chen, Chief Research Officer at OpenAI, argues that pre-training remains crucial and underappreciated in scaling next-generation AI. He emphasizes the importance of real-world applications and innovation.

### 🔑 Key Insights
*   **Pre-Training is Underrated:** Mark Chen asserts that pre-training techniques remain essential for unlocking future advancements.
*   **Real-World Applications Matter:** He discusses the challenges faced by models in subjective fields like creative writing, highlighting the need for nuanced approaches.
*   **Innovation Through Scaling:** Chen emphasizes the importance of continuous scaling and innovation to push AI towards AGI.

### 🧠 Deep Dive
#### **The Role of Pre-Training**
Mark Chen believes that pre-training is not dead but rather underrated. "The narrative only started spreading more widely after around the last one or two years," he says, noting that this technique has been a key driver in scaling AI over decades. He argues that careful research and engineering can consistently break through bottlenecks, allowing for further scaling.

#### **Real-World Challenges**
Chen addresses the challenges faced by models in subjective fields such as creative writing. "Creative writing is an area where you can take two pieces of work and get wildly different opinions," he explains. This highlights the need for models to handle context and human-like reasoning, which are currently more challenging than objective tasks.

#### **Innovation Through Scaling**
Chen discusses how OpenAI focuses on continuous scaling and innovation. "We're getting closer and closer to a world where the models can come up with more of the innovations on their own," he says. This suggests that as AI capabilities grow, they will increasingly be capable of self-sustained research.
