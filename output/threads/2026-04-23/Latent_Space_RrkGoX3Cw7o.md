## 📰 SUMMARY ARTICLE

# Beyond the Transformer: How Shopify is Engineering the Future with 100% AI Adoption

**TL;DR:** Shopify CTO Mikhail Parakhin reveals a radical new engineering paradigm: moving away from manual A/B testing toward large-scale customer simulations (SimGym), utilizing auto-research loops (Tangent) to automate experimentation, and adopting Liquid Neural Networks for ultra-low latency search.

### 🔑 Key Insights
* **The Token Philosophy:** Shifting from "cost-saving" to "value-maximizing" by using high-quality models for intensive critique loops rather than just mass-producing code.
* **Automated Research:** The implementation of "Tangent," an agentic loop that performs "hill climbing" across hundreds of experiments to find optimal configurations.
* **Simulated Economies:** Using "SimGym" to run counterfactual simulations on historical data, allowing merchants to predict conversion changes without real-world risk.
* **Architectural Evolution:** The transition toward Liquid Neural Networks to achieve sub-30ms latency for complex, high-frequency search queries.

### 🧠 Deep Dive

#### The End of Manual Experimentation
For decades, the gold standard of software and product development has been the A/B test. However, as Mikhail Parakhimate explains, the sheer volume of code and data generated by AI makes traditional testing a bottleneck. Shopify is moving toward "Auto-Research" via their internal tool, **Tangent**. This system allows engineers to set a goal (a loss function) and let agents run hundreds of iterations—essentially performing "digital archaeology" and "hill climbing" simultaneously. The result is an automated pipeline where the machine finds the optimal solution while the human provides the high-level direction.

#### Simulating the Human Element
One of the most profound shifts discussed is **SimGym**. Traditional A/B testing requires real traffic and time, which small merchants often cannot afford. SimGym leverages Shopify's decades of historical transaction data to create a "digital twin" of customer behavior. By running counterfactual simulations—asking "what if we changed this button color?"—Shopify can predict the impact on "add-to-cart" events with high correlation to reality. This transforms the merchant experience from reactive testing to proactive, predictive optimization.

#### The Architecture of Efficiency
While Transformers dominate the current landscape, they face scalability issues regarding context length and latency. Shopify is actively integrating **Liquid Neural Networks** for high-frequency workloads like search. Because these models are sub-quadratic and highly efficient, they can provide deep query understanding and personalization in under 30 milliseconds. This move represents a broader industry trend: the transition from massive, general-purpose models to specialized, highly efficient architectures optimized for the "edge" of production.

#### The New Engineering Bottleneck
As AI-native engineering reaches 100% adoption, the bottleneck has shifted from *writing* code to *verifying* it. The explosion of code volume threatens to overwhelm CI/CD pipelines and increase bug rates. Parakhin emphasizes that the "anti-pattern" is a swarm of uncoordinated agents. The future lies in "critique loops"—where high-quality, expensive models act as rigorous reviewers, ensuring that the sheer volume of AI-generated code does not compromise system stability.

### 💬 Notable Quotes
> "The real problem is not in spending time waiting for PR; the real problem is since there's so much more code, the probability of at least some tests failing goes up... you have to have a very rigorous PR review also automated, of course."

> "If you are not using an auto-research approach in whatever you do, then you're missing out."

### 🏁 Conclusion
The transition to AI-native engineering at Shopify is more than just a tool upgrade; it is a fundamental reimagining of the software development lifecycle. By treating intelligence as a scalable, programmable resource—and leveraging proprietary data to simulate the real world—Shopify is building a moat that is increasingly impossible for competitors to replicate through models alone.
