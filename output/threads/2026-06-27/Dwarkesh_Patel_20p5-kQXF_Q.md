# The Next Training Paradigm: AGI's Horizon and the Quest for General Intelligence

**TL;DR:** The next training paradigm is betting on scalable reinforcement learning (RL) to solve a myriad of tasks across diverse environments, aiming to create an agent that can generalize from short-term tasks to real-world success. However, key challenges like sample efficiency and continual learning must be addressed.

### 🔑 Key Insights
*   **Generalization from Short-Horizon Tasks:** The labs are betting on RLVR (Reinforcement Learning from Virtual Rollouts) to train AIs that can generalize their skills to long-term real-world tasks.
*   **Sample Efficiency vs. Continual Learning:** Sample efficiency during training and continual learning in deployment are critical, but both pose significant challenges for AI development.
*   **Context Length and Generalization:** There's skepticism about whether short-horizon RL training generalizes well to long-horizon performance.

### 🧠 Deep Dive

#### The Bet on Scalable Reinforcement Learning
The video essay discusses a research bet that scaling reinforcement learning (RL) across millions of tasks in diverse environments will result in AGI. Optimists argue that this approach can overcome current deficits like data inefficiency and lack of continual learning, similar to how more compute solved natural language processing (NLP).

#### Sample Efficiency: A Key Challenge
AIs trained on RLVR are one-millionth as sample-efficient as humans during training. This means they require vast amounts of data and computation. For domains where real-world interaction is necessary (like coding), this can be managed, but for others (like politics or business), it's more challenging.

#### Continual Learning: The Missing Link
Continual learning in deployment is crucial because the most valuable information is revealed during real-world use. Current AIs struggle with this due to the lack of a clean separation between training and inference, where weights are not updated effectively.

#### Dreaming & OPSD: New Approaches
Two speculative ideas—dreaming (building simulations for rehearsal) and on-policy self-distillation (OPSD, distilling learning back into the base model)—are proposed as potential solutions to these challenges. These approaches aim to provide more efficient use of real-world data while training.

#### The Future of AI: AGI or Incremental Improvement?
The essay concludes by exploring how continual learning and RLVR might combine to create an AI that can generalize from short-term tasks to long-term success, potentially leading to AGI. However, the current limitations in sample efficiency and continual learning suggest that significant breakthroughs are needed.

### 💬 Notable Quotes
> "There's two things: there's the context length you train at, and there's a context length that you serve at. If you train at a small context length and then try to serve at a long context length, maybe you get these degradations." - Dario Amodei

### 🏁 Conclusion
The next training paradigm for AGI is a high-stakes bet on scalable RLVR, but the road ahead is fraught with challenges. As AI research continues, the key will be finding ways to improve sample efficiency and continual learning so that AIs can truly generalize their skills across diverse domains.
