## 📰 SUMMARY ARTICLE

# Breaking Benchmarks: How Modern AI Models Need More Test Time Compute for True Evaluation

**TL;DR:** Traditional benchmarking methods fail to accurately measure modern AI models' true capabilities, as they do not account for the varying budgets and test time compute required. Noam Brown from OpenAI argues that evaluating models at different budget levels is crucial.

### 🔑 Key Insights
*   **Budget Dependency:** Model performance significantly improves with increased test time compute.
*   **Benchmarking Revisions Needed:** Current benchmarks should include an X-axis of cost or tokens to reflect true capabilities.
*   **Long-Term Evaluation:** Running models for months, not just days, is essential for a full understanding of their potential.

### 🧠 Deep Dive
**Budget Dependency in Model Performance**
Noam Brown highlights the critical issue that the capability of modern AI models is directly tied to their budget. For instance, a model given $10 million can perform far better than one with just $10,000. This means traditional benchmarks, which often compare models at fixed settings, fail to capture their true potential.

**Revising Benchmarking Methods**
Brown suggests that the industry should adopt more comprehensive evaluation methods. He proposes plotting performance as a function of test time compute or cost. For example, benchmarks could show how model performance improves with increasing budget levels, providing a clearer picture of real-world capabilities.

**Long-Term Evaluation and Real-World Use Cases**
While short-term benchmarking can give some insights, Brown argues that long-term evaluations are necessary to fully understand a model's potential. He uses the poker bot example, where running models for weeks revealed significant improvements not visible in shorter tests. This underscores the importance of real-world use cases in evaluating AI models.

**Routing and Consensus Among Models**
Brown also discusses how routing and consensus among multiple models can enhance performance but only if they are given equivalent budgets. Current methods that claim routing improves performance without accounting for budget discrepancies are misleading.

### 💬 Notable Quotes
> "The capability of the model is a function of how much money you put into it. Basically, if you give it a budget of $10,000, it can do a lot more than what it can do with a budget of $10." - Noam Brown

### 🏁 Conclusion
As AI models continue to evolve and become more powerful, the need for accurate evaluation methods becomes ever more critical. By adopting new benchmarking practices that account for varying budgets and test time compute, we can better understand and harness the full potential of these advanced technologies.
