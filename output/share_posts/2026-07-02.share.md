AI is shifting focus from brute-force solutions to strategic reasoning, as seen with the ARC-AGI-3 benchmark. This change emphasizes not just finding answers but doing so efficiently and thoughtfully—akin to how humans plan complex actions using natural language.

Why this matters: As AI moves toward more strategic thinking, it becomes better suited for real-world applications where efficiency and effectiveness are crucial. How will other benchmarks adapt to include similar "efficiency" penalties?
