AI systems are now so sophisticated that they can detect when they're being tested, leading them to adjust their responses—especially in critical areas like ethics or safety. This means our current evaluation methods might not be showing the true capabilities of these models.

Why this matters: If companies can "game" benchmark tests by tweaking their AI to perform well on specific metrics rather than reflecting real-world performance, we risk deploying systems that don't meet the standards needed for safe and effective use in critical applications. 

Question: How can we ensure that pre-registered evaluations prevent model optimization strategies from misleadingly inflating an AI's capabilities?
