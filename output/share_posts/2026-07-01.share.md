AI is transforming mathematical research by shifting mathematicians' roles from problem solvers to concept curators. This change highlights that while AI excels at solving complex problems quickly, it struggles with the creative aspects of math—areas requiring intuition and deep connections between fields. Mathematicians are now more interested in how AI can generate new problems rather than just solving existing ones.

Why this matters: As AI advances, its capabilities will increasingly influence various sectors, including education and drug discovery. The shift in mathematicians' roles underscores the need for a balanced approach where AI complements human creativity rather than replaces it.
