AI is on the cusp of transforming our economies and societies, yet political leaders often lack a deep understanding of these technologies. As Jeremy Grantham warns investors to avoid US stocks and crypto, suggesting a shift towards foreign markets and real estate, it's clear that financial markets are already reacting to potential risks. This cautionary tale should prompt us to question whether our political systems are prepared for the challenges and opportunities AI presents.

Meanwhile, Dex Hunter-Torricke argues that current governance frameworks focus too narrowly on technical solutions, neglecting broader societal implications. His assertion that "the vast majority of decision-makers have a wafer-thin understanding or interest in any of these technologies" highlights a critical gap. How can we bridge this knowledge gap and ensure that AI is developed and deployed responsibly?
