AI models today face a "data black hole," needing trillions of tokens to learn what humans master with just 200 million words. This gap, combined with tax incentives favoring automation, is set to drastically reshape the job market within years, particularly eliminating entry-level white-collar roles.

Why this matters: As we race toward AGI, the economic landscape is shifting dramatically, forcing us to rethink education and career paths. How will new graduates adapt when traditional learning jobs are replaced by machines?
