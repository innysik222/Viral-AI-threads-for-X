AI is no longer just assisting doctors; it's taking on primary roles in diagnosing conditions like tuberculosis and lung cancer. Qure.ai’s automated system processes 15 million X-rays annually, providing immediate results that can be acted upon within seconds, especially in regions where radiologists are scarce.

This shift could dramatically reduce diagnostic delays and scale healthcare access. But how will doctors and patients react when AI is the first point of contact for critical health issues? Will trust in these systems grow as they prove their reliability, or will there be concerns about the human touch missing from initial consultations?
